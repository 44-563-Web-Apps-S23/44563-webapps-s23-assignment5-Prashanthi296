RESTIO

Restio is a genus of flowering plants within the family Restionaceae, described in 1772.[5][3] The entire genus is endemic to South Africa (Cape Province and KwaZulu-Natal).[4] In common with a number of other genera in the Restionaceae, restios are widely cultivated for use as garden ornamentals for their attractive nodular foliage

ABELIA

Abelia /əˈbiːliə/[1] is a previously recognized genus that contained about 30 species and hybrids, placed in the honeysuckle family, Caprifoliaceae. Molecular phylogenetic studies showed that the genus was not monophyletic, and in 2013, Maarten Christenhusz proposed the merger of Abelia (excluding section Zabelia) into Linnaea, along with some other genera. Abelia section Zabelia was raised to the genus Zabelia.[2]
